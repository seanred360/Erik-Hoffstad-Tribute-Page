Eric Hoffstad

Teaching proper internet comment etiquette

Youtube Series:

  • Lessons - The lessons are the main bulk of his Youtube channel. In this series, Erik teaches his audience how to type "proper" and "respectful" comments online. He does this by watching various videos of a certain topic or theme and typing a humorous comment on each. The purpose is to point out and poke fun at how people behave on the internet.
  • Tweets From the Class - Tweets From the Class is a series in which Erik comments on or answers any questions from tweets sent to him. These are usually troll comments by fans seeking a humorous response. Each episode usually ends with an offensive tweet to Ted Cruz, or any political figure relevant at the time of the video.
  • Big Money Salvia Reacts - This can hardly be considered a series since Erik only did one reaction video of him watching the "Batman vs Superman" trailer in 2016.
  • GAME TIPZ - GAME TIPZ is a series in which Erik gives satirical advice on how to perform better in various video games. These "tips" often involve the killing of his own teammates.
  • Let's Get Into the Middle of It with Erik - This short-lived series consists of only two videos in 2015. The series consists of Erik trolling people involved in controversial current events and poking fun at the public's reaction in the comment sections online.
  • Subreddit of the Week - This series involves Erik showing his viewers a weird or controversial subreddit on the popular website, Reddit. Erik typically leaves satirical posts on these subreddits that attempt to disrupt the unethical activities the users are engaging in. (Such as a subreddit dedicated to the celebration of beating women)
  • Erik Drinks Wine and Gets a Haircut - In the video, "Internet Comment Etiquette: Crowd Funding", Erik created a Kickstarter to make a film about a man who drinks wine and gets a haircut. This was intended as satire to poke fun at the platform and some of the ridiculous things that get funded. To Erik's surprise, the Kickstarter raised $4,227 and a contributor got to choose a pineapple haircut for Erik.

"The muck of the internet is my classroom. I'll show you how to stay clean."
